Mathematics Foundation

Mathematics forms a crucial part of the exam, strictly carrying a massive weightage every single year. It consists of two distinct components:

  • Engineering Mathematics (4–6 Marks): Linear Algebra (Eigenvalues, Determinants), Calculus (Maxima/Minima), and Probability distributions.
  • Discrete Mathematics (8–10 Marks): Propositional Logic, Graph Theory, Combinatorics, and Set Theory. This subject also builds the foundation for TOC and Algorithms.

General Aptitude

General Aptitude is a fixed 15 Marks section across all GATE papers. It comprises 5 questions of 1 mark each and 5 questions of 2 marks each. Ignoring aptitude is the biggest mistake candidates make. Strong quantitative and verbal skills here provide a massive competitive edge.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Which subject has the highest weightage in GATE CS?

A: Historically, Programming and Data Structures, along with Discrete & Engineering Mathematics, carry the highest weightage (often exceeding 12-15 marks each).

Q: Is it safe to skip any subject for GATE CS?

A: It is highly risky to skip subjects entirely. Even low-weightage subjects like Compiler Design (4-6 marks) feature straightforward, high-scoring questions. Skipping them can cost you crucial ranks.

Q: How many marks are allocated to General Aptitude?

A: General Aptitude carries a strict 15 marks (10 questions) in all GATE papers, making it effectively the single highest-weighted 'subject' area in the exam.
